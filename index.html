Nina The Clownfish

Species: Ocellaris

Length: 11 cm (4.3 inches)

Feeding and Foods:

  • Feed adults twice a day and juveniles 3 to 4 times a day
  • Crustaceans (such as copepods and amphipods)
  • Algae
  • Polychaete Worms
  • Leftovers from the anemone's meal

Eartha The Freshwater African Butterflyfish

Species: Buchholzi

Length: 12.95 cm (5.1 inches)

Feeding and Foods:

  • Several feedings per day
  • Feed flies
  • Mosquito Larvae
  • Small Spiders
  • Worms
  • Small Fish
  • Brine Shrimp

Josephine The Betta

Species: Splendens

Length: 7.01 cm (2.8 in)

Feeding and Foods:

  • Generally feed once or twice a day
  • Zooplankton
  • Insect Larvae
  • Crustaceans
  • Aquatic Insects
  • Brine Shrimp
  • Blood Worms

Zora The Seahorse

Species: Hippocampus spp

Length: 5 to 36 cm (2 to 14 in)

Feeding and Foods:

  • Feed 2-3 times daily
  • Plankton
  • Fish Larvae

How to Capture These Species

How to harvest a Clownfish:

Find a clean, clear container that is large enough to hold the fish or pair in question. You can buy clear plastic specimen containers at better stocked LFS or online. These are ideal, but anything that is clear, has a wide opening and is not easily broken will work, the key here is clear.
You will need both hands for the capture. Place the clear container in the tank near the fish. Hold it in place with one hand and use your other hand to herd the fish into the container. Most clownfish if you move slowly and carefully will not flee and can be herded into the container and lifted out of the tank.
If your fish is a runner... Place the container in an open corner of the tank and herd the fish into the corner and container. This is where having a rectangular specimen container is handy, no gaps for escapes.
When you carry or move the fish in the clear container it is a good idea to cover the top and bottom. Scared clownfish tend to jump so having something on top to keep them in the container is nice. Covering the bottom will help to not scare them with suddenly moving floor.

Locations

Josephine was harvested in Vietman's Yen Stream.

Zora was harvested in Barbados Cost.

Clownfish Maintence

Clean Frequency: Regular water changes done bi-weekly will also help replace the trace elements that the fish and corals use up.

Salinity: 1.023-1.025 SG

Temperature: 23.3 to 27.8° C (74.0 to 82.0° F)

Freshwater Maintence

Clean Frequency: A weekly water change of 15-20% is the standard recommendation.

Salinity: 100mg salt per litre (0.0035 oz per 0.26 gal)

Temperature: 25.0 to 27.8° C (77.0 to 82.0° F)

Betta Maintence

Clean Frequency: Regular water changes are a must with 25% weekly being recommended.

Salinity: 1 tablespoon per 5 gallons

Temperature: 23.9 to 30.0° C (75.0 to 86.0° F)

Seahorse Maintenance

Clean Frequency: Regular water changes 10-20% percent water change in your tank every 1-2 weeks.

Salinity: 1.020-1.025 SG

Temperature: 21.1 to 23.3° C (70.0 to 74.0° F)
